This Cookie Policy describes how Pathway To Dental Success recognizes you when you visit this website at https://pathwaytodentalsuccess.me using cookies and other similar technologies. It explains what these technologies are, why they are used, and how you might limit the use of them.

Cookies may be used to gather personal information in some situations, or that information may become personal information if it is combined with other data.

What are cookies?

When you visit a website, small data files called cookies are stored on your computer or mobile device. Cookies are commonly used by website owners to help their websites work more efficiently and to generate reporting data.

First party cookies are those placed by the website owner (in this example, Pathway To Dental Success). Third-party cookies are those that are placed by parties other than the website's owner. Third-party cookies allow for the implementation of third-party features or functionality on or through the website (e.g. like advertising, interactive content and analytics). The parties who place these third-party cookies are able to recognize your computer both when it visits the website in issue and when it visits other websites.

This website employs both first-party and third-party cookies. These cookies allow for tracking and targeting users' interests to improve their experience on our Online Properties. Third-party cookies are placed by third parties on this website for advertising, analytics, and other uses. Does this website use web beacons?

Cookies are not the only means to identify and track website visitors. Other similar technologies, such as web beacons, may be used from time to time (sometimes called "tracking pixels" or "clear gifs"). These are small graphical files with a unique identifier for recognition of visitations of this website. This enables for tracking user traffic patterns from one website page to the next, deliver or communicate with cookies, determine whether you arrived at the website via an online advertisement displayed on a third-party website, improve site performance, and track the success of e-mail marketing campaigns. In many cases, these technologies rely on cookies to work effectively. Thus, refusing cookies will make them less functional.

Does this website use Flash cookies or Local Shared Objects?

Websites may use "Flash Cookies" (also known as Local Shared Objects or "LSOs") to collect and store information about your use of this website, fraud protection, and other site operations.

If you do not want Flash Cookies to be stored on your computer, you can use the tools in the Website Storage Settings Panel to change the settings of your Flash player to prevent Flash Cookies from being recorded. You can also manage Flash Cookies by going to the Global Storage Settings Panel and following the instructions (which may include instructions on how to delete existing Flash Cookies (referred to as "information" on the Macromedia site), how to prevent Flash LSOs from being installed on your computer without your permission, and (for Flash Player 8 and later) how to block Flash Cookies that are not being delivered by the page's operator.

Please be aware that restricting or limiting Flash Cookie acceptance in the Flash Player may impair or prevent the functionality of various Flash applications, including Flash applications used in connection with our services or online content.

Does this website serve targeted advertising?

To offer advertising through our Websites, third parties may place cookies on your computer or mobile device. These businesses may use information about your visits to this and other websites to target you with ads for goods and services you might be interested in. They might also use technology that measures the efficiency of adverts. This can be performed by their collecting information about your visits to this and other sites using cookies or web beacons in order to present relevant adverts about goods and services that may be of interest to you. Unless you choose otherwise, the information acquired through this method does not allow us or them to identify your name, contact information, or other details that directly identify you.
