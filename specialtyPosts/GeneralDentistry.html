Types of Dental Specialties: General Dentistry

Welcome to the Types of Dental Specialties series! In this article, we will be focusing on General Dentistry.

What does a general dentist do?

Welcome to the first installment of the Types of Dental Specialties series! We'll be talking about general dentistry in this article.

The majority of people have visited a general dentist at least twice a year. Typically, general dentists assess a patient's entire oral health. Every 2-3 years the dental staff will take an x-ray to detect any abnormalities in the mouth. If a tooth starts decaying, the dentist will undertake procedures such as dental fillings and crowns. In more extreme instances, some general dentists will perform root canals or place implants, if they are comfortable doing so. Otherwise, the patient will be referred to a specialist. General dentists can also provide routine cleanings if they desire, or they can hire a hygienist to do so.

General dentists are permitted to perform all operations learned in dental school. They can do only fillings and crowns if that's all they want to do. If they want to do more procedures, they can do that, as well. Some general dentists are even certified to administer botox or provide invisalign.

What is a general dentist's salary?

General dentists earn an average of $156,000 per year. However, in other locations, they might earn up to $278,000 per year. Dentists who make money on the higher end will be found in cities with a low dentist-to-population ratio. Cities with a larger dentist-to-population ratio, on the other hand, will have dentists who make money on the lower end of the scale.

Dentists who own their own practice have a better chance of making a larger net income. Running a practice, on the other hand, is quite challenging and demands a significant amount of extra time and work on the part of the individual who is both a dentist and a business owner. If they are not successful, they may earn less money than an associate working for someone else, or they may even lose money. Before starting a practice or taking over an existing one, it's critical to find a competent mentor who can teach how to manage a successful practice.
